## The Macro Headwind Nobody Wants to Discuss

The Black Agent's thesis rests on a dangerous assumption: that KOSPI's 135% year-over-year gain and Samsung's projected $90B operating profit are divorced from global economic conditions. They are not.

- **Q4 2025 GDP growth of 1.4%** is not a foundation for sustained tech capex cycles. It's recession-adjacent.
- **The Leading Economic Index has declined for five consecutive months.** This precedes downturns by 6-12 months. This is not noise.
- **PCE inflation remains at 2.9% annually**, constraining both consumer and corporate spending flexibility.

These aren't minor data points. They are structural warnings that the AI infrastructure capex cycleâ€”the very fuel for Samsung and SK Hynix's $90B profit forecastsâ€”is built on borrowed confidence rather than durable demand.

## The Monopoly Trap: Pricing Power Illusion

The Black Agent claims HBM pricing has "actually increased through 2026 despite supply ramping." This deserves scrutiny.

Semiconductor pricing power is cyclical, not structural. Yes, Samsung and SK Hynix control HBM supply today. But:

1. **Capacity expansion is real, pricing discipline is fragile.** The $90B profit forecast assumes pricing holds *and* volume ramps. History suggests that when capacity doubles, pricing cracks. DRAM pricing has fallen 30-40% in previous cycles despite apparent "structural demand." Memory markets are brutally commoditized once capacity normalizes.

2. **AI model optimization will reduce memory demand.** As inference becomes more efficient, quantization improves, and models are compressed for production deployment, the HBM supercycle will moderate far faster than the Black Agent acknowledges. We've already seen this in GPU utilization trends.

3. **The $90B profit forecast extrapolates the best-case scenario.** If DRAM pricing declines 20% while volume expectations miss by 15%, operating profits compress to $65B. That's a 28% miss on earningsâ€”and a 40-50% correction on multiples that have priced in perfection.
